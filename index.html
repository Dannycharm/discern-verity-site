The problem

Fleets collect safety data every shift.
At renewal, it counts for nothing.

Small and mid-sized trucking fleets are already running Samsara, Motive, or Geotab. The cameras detect. The platform scores. Alerts go out. The dashboard fills up. And then renewal arrives, and the underwriter prices the account on three to five years of loss history — because that's all they have.

Premiums keep rising even for clean fleets, because an undocumented safety program is invisible to the market. The fleet with no paper trail and the fleet with a disciplined, year-round program look identical from the outside. That gap is where premium dollars disappear.

The telematics platform works for the software vendor. The insurer's loss-control team works for the carrier. The fleet owner is usually the safety director too — between dispatch and payroll. Nobody in that room is building the fleet's case for a lower premium. That's the job Discern Verity does.

What I deliver

Three things that stack into one renewal argument.

Step 1

Driver Scorecard

Every driver scored weekly on a 0–100 scale, normalized per 1,000 miles with severity weights tied to crash correlation. Every number is traceable to the raw events that produced it — no black box. Four-week rolling trends make coaching progress visible.

Step 2

Documented Coaching

The difference between telling an underwriter "we coach our drivers" and showing a driver's measured improvement over a quarter. Before-and-after trajectories, session records, and the event breakdown that explains each score — assembled in a format underwriters recognize.

Step 3

Renewal Packet

An underwriter-ready PDF that tells the fleet's risk story, presents the current-year trend, and documents the safety program in the language that moves premiums. Portable — the same packet works at every market the broker shops, not just the incumbent.

How it cuts the renewal

Three layers of leverage that work simultaneously.

A well-documented renewal packet doesn't rely on one argument. It creates pressure from three directions at once.

Layer 1

The Loss Story

A documented trend of measurable improvement in weighted events per 1,000 miles is placed directly in the renewal file. It contradicts the loss history the underwriter is pricing with current-year data they wouldn't otherwise see.

Layer 2

Schedule Rating Credits

Most commercial auto policies allow ±15–25% discretionary adjustments for safety organization, driver training, and management cooperation. These credits are documentation-triggered — an underwriter can't grant them on a fleet's word. The packet is the paperwork that unlocks them.

Layer 3

Carrier Telematics Programs

Progressive, Northland, Nationwide, and newer trucking insurers (Nirvana, HDVI, Cover Whale) all discount on verified telematics data. A fleet presenting analyzed, documented safety data gets access to programs the carrier's own loss-control team won't proactively surface.
